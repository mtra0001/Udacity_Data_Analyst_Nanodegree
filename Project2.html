Introduction¶

Overview¶

As part of the Data Analysis programme I am going to investigate a Soccer Dataset which is made up of six different tables containing data for soccer matches, players, and teams form several European Countries from 2008 to 2016.

The investigation will be based on the following questions:

  1. Which are the best free kick takers - Left or Right footed players?
  2. Is there a relationship between acceleration, height and weight?
  3. Which teams got the most points per game between the 2012/13 - 2015/16 seasons?

Conclusions¶

Question 1

From Question 1 we concluded that left footed players have a better free kick accuracy than right footed players. One limitation from this quick research was that I had duplicate data and didn't consider instances where certain players improve their free kick score.

Traditionally free kick takers are usually also very good at long shots and therefore it is was worth finding out the correlation between both variables. My research concluded a strong correlation between free kick taking and long shooting. It was noticed that some players have a good shooting ability but a very poor free kick accuracy. This data could be effected by the fact that these players don't usually take free kicks due to not playing or simply they are not regular free kick takers.

Question 2

In research Question 2 I tried to find a relationship between acceleration, height and weight. From the data it was evident that a strong correlation exists between height and weight which is understandable. On the other hand, the relationship between acceleration and height/weight was moderate.

Taking other variables into consideration such as the playing position might also answer why certain players have lower acceleration than average. For example acceleration for GKs is not a priority and therefore this data is more prone to be inaccurate.

Question 3

In my final question I found out the best teams based on points per game during the 2012/13 - 2015/16 seasons are: FC Bayern Munich, SL Benfica, FC Barcelona, Juventus and Celtic.

I only considered teams that played a minimum of 15 games per season for each of the 4 different seasons.
